Swanston Library

Service Design

Client: RMIT University

This proposal was selected
to present in Swanston Library in 2017.

In 2017, xyxy and her team member, Nick, used one semester time to find the problems in Swanston Library, define and solve the main one that they focused, which is the wayfinding problem.

Instead of providing the solutions directly, xyxy and Nick took a step back, they thought about “what is a library”, and what information Swanston Library wants to tell. The Swanston Library is an institution steeped in values of discovery, shared knowledge, and support service to name a few. In this project, their job isn’t to design a surface that reflects those values to the library community. Their job is to create a system that embodies and uses those values throughout a series of participatory design tasks.

Quite simply, those values are put to direct use. They are values in action.

Her shining moments:

xyxy was the illustrator and concept artist in this project. Finding the human element in every story and bringing it to life on the page is her biggest passion. It is also quite obvious that she is an avid music lover.
