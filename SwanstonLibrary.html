Swanston Library

Service Design

Client: RMIT University

Direction: Research
Interview
Data Analysis
Prototype
Presentation

This proposal was selected
to present in Swanston Library in 2017.

In 2017, my team member, Nick, and I used one semester time to find the problems in Swanston Library, define and solve the main one that they focused, which is the wayfinding problem.

Instead of providing the solutions directly, Nick and I took a step back to think about “what is a library”, and what information Swanston Library wants to tell. The Swanston Library is an institution steeped in values of discovery, shared knowledge, and support service to name a few. In this project, our job isn’t to design a surface that reflects those values to the library community. Our job is to create a system that embodies and uses those values throughout a series of participatory design tasks.

Quite simply, those values are put to direct use. They are values in action.

My shining moments:

I was the illustrator and concept artist in this project. Finding the human element in every story and bringing it to life on the page is my biggest passion. It is also quite obvious that I am an avid music lover and what I love inspired my works.
