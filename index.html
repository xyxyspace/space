What & how & why,
find the reasons behind...


xyxy has the habit of watching people, she likes to be an observer. Feelings, emotions and behaviours are the things xyxy values the most. Though service design is not that new to xyxy, she always keep learning while working with different people in different cases.

In most of the projects, she always takes the role of finding out the reasons behind and an active methods provider. She also uses her communication design skills to let more people understand the ideas, let them stand out.

That is why she now works with Metro Trains & Paddl Company.
xyxy learned a lot about service design and UI/UX design from her teachers, leaders and team members like Nick and Harry in the projects with EY, RMIT Swanston Library and AusPost.


Save Spaceship Earth,
get on board to the universe!


Jigsaw Factory is in the form of the designers' youth idealism, but the thoughts and spirit behind it is way far ahead of the time. Many people, especially people out of Australia, are not that familiar with this magical company.

How to introduce Jigsaw Factory to the public? How to build the connection between this company and the audience today? How to make everyone the hero? Those questions led xyxy and her teams to explore the meaning and possibilities of the exhibition, and also bring the concepts down to the Earth.

Believe in your superpower and everyone is the hero!

This is an exhibition curated by RMIT Master of Communication Design students in collaboration with the RMIT Design Archives. The exbition was on from 11-18 Oct, 2018.

Read read read,
oh you can make it...


Do you spend a long time reading articles and books?

To be honest, she can't. But you can find all the clues about how she tried to read an article in this little book, My Journey to the Centre of Google Earth.

We all know the death of print has been falsely predicted for over a century now, it's undeniable the effect the last 30 years has had on the form of the book and the mechanisms of publishing. So what will the book be in the future? Will people still read?
A book designed for the 2018 NGV Melbourne Art Book Fair, referencing the 2012 book Post-Digital Print: The Mutation of Publishing since 1894, by Alessandro Ludovico. Books are pocketbook sized (107.9mm x 174.6mm), 210 pages, printed in black on white paper and perfect bound.

One & two & three,
starts from ABC...


How do people understand alphabet?

When we say "A", "B", "C", we absolutely know the order. So if we make a list, instead of using "one", "two", "three", can people still realize everything is in the order?

That is definitely not because she has too many daily pieces to collect and experimentally tried to combine them together.

A limited printing book designed for the 2018 NGV Melbourne Art Book Fair. Books are small sized (74mm x 148.5mm), 30 pages, printed in black on white and yellow paper, and round staple bound.
